Talking The Talk

Preparation Round: Introduction (10 minutes): The students will be introduced to the concept of news reporting, different styles (factual, investigative), and the importance of accuracy and clarity.
Group Formation (5 minutes): Students will be divided into groups of 3-4.
News Source Selection (10 minutes): They will be provided with a list of reliable news sources. Groups will browse the list and choose a story that interests them.
Role Assignment (5 minutes): Within each group, students will assign roles: Reporter, Anchor (introduces and concludes the report), and Interviewee (optional, for stories that benefit from an interview format).
Research and Scriptwriting (40 minutes): Groups will research their chosen story using the provided sources. They will then collaborate on writing a news report script (5-7 minutes) focusing on accuracy, clarity, and proper sentence structure.

Practice Time (20 minutes): Groups will have dedicated time to rehearse their news reports, focusing on clear and engaging delivery.
Presentation (30 minutes): Each group will present their news report in front of the workshop audience. The Reporter will deliver the news report, while the Anchor will introduce and conclude it. The Interviewee will participate if the story format includes one.
Feedback (10 minutes): After each presentation, the facilitator will provide constructive feedback on the group's report, focusing on accuracy, clarity, and delivery.

Speak-Up

Competition Round:
Starting Sentence (5 minutes): The competition facilitator will reveal the starting sentence for all groups, which will be a clear ending point for the story.
Backwards Storytelling (30 minutes): Groups will take turns adding one sentence to their story, going backward in time. Each group will have a set time limit per turn (e.g., 30 seconds) to come up with and add their sentence.
Focus: Sentences should be creative and connect logically to the previous sentence, building a cohesive backwards story.

Judging-Criteria Creativity: Originality and imaginative use of language.

Transitions: Sentences seamlessly connect to each other, creating a smooth narrative flow.
Delivery (Optional): If groups choose to present their stories verbally, delivery will be considered (clear pronunciation, engaging tone)

Basketball

It is a team sport that involves two teams of five active players each trying to score points against one another by throwing a ball through a 300 cm (10 feet) high hoop (the 'basket') under organised rules.It is played between 10 players which consist of 5 players each from both teams
Rules and Regulations :- 1. The player must bounce, or dribble, the ball with one hand while moving both feet.
2. Basketball players cannot kick the ball or hit it with their fist.
3. No player can touch the basketball while it is traveling downward towards the basket or if it is on the rim. This is called goaltending. (touching the ball on the rim is legal in some games).
4. Every player on the court is subject to the same rules regardless of the position they play. The positions in basketball are just for team basketball strategy and there are no positions in the rules.
5. The referee's decision is the final decision throughout the game. Any kind of misbehavior will lead to disqualification of the team.

Chess

Chess is an abstract strategy game. It is played on a square chessboard with 64 squares arranged in an eight-by-eight grid. At the start, each player (one controlling the white pieces, the other controlling the black pieces) controls sixteen pieces: one king, one queen, two rooks, two knights, two bishops, and eight pawns. The object of the game is to checkmate the opponent’s king, whereby the king is under immediate attack and there is no way to remove it from attack on the next move.

Team Composition:- 1 player in a team.
Rules and regulations: 1. Tournament format will be finalized after the possible number of participants is determined.
2. Understand Check and the ways to get out of check (move, block, capture).
3. Understand the “touch-move” rule.
4. Understand 50 moves draw rule i.e. the game can be claimed drawn after 50 moves if there are no pawn moves or no captures.
5. The game can be claimed drawn if the exact same position is repeated thrice.
6. No outside assistance during games is allowed.
7. Judges decision is final.

Table Tennis

Table tennis is a sport in which two or four players hit a lightweight ball, also known as the ping-pong ball, back and forth across a table using small solid rackets. The game takes place on a hard table divided by a net. Team composition: Members: 1player Rules and regulations: Game is played to 11 points.
Players serve two serves each,alternating.
A player does not have to win specifically off their own serve in order to win a point.
If a game ties at 10-10, a player must win by 2 points.
In this situation, players serve one serve each, alternating.
You must throw the ball up straight, from a flat palm, at least 6inches (16cm).
Your toss and service contact must be behind the table surface (not over).
If the ball hits the net during service, it is a let, the point is replayed.
There is no limit or point deductions for let serves.
10. Proper decorum is mandatory and judges decision is final.
